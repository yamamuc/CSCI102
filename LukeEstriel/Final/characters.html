Assuming you want to play a caster, just pick one at random, right?

Ohoho! What a fool you are!

Yes, there are many classes, and each has different roles, in and out of combat.
We will focus on three classes for proof of concept: Cleric, Sorcerer, and Wizard.
Here is a generic table showcasing their basic capabilities.

Class Name Defensive Capacity Offensive Capacity Healing Capacity
Cleric Average Weak Strong
Sorcerer Average Strong Weak
Wizard Strong Average Weak

This isn't a perfect representation, but hopefully you get the gist.

The important thing to consider foremost is "what do I want to be able to do?" If you enjoy playing healers in other games,
consider playing the Cleric. If you're one for combos and strategizing, the Sorcerer is a perfect pick. If you enjoy playing blue
decks in Magic the Gathering, the Wizard might be for you.

As well, these three rely one different Abilities to cast their magic.

Clerics rely on Wisdom, often associated with Senses and "Street Smarts".
Sorcerers rely on Charisma, often associated with Demeanor and Charm (the social kind).
Wizards rely on Intelligence, often associated with Memory and "Book Smarts".

Do consider picking a caster that suits your personal strengths. It can be easier to understand the character this way.
